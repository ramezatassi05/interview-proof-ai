## 2. THE PROBLEM WE SOLVE (Pain Points for Content)

These are the real emotional pain points your content should tap into. Every single one of these is a TikTok/Reel hook:

### The Core Pain

- **You never find out why you got rejected.** Companies ghost you. HR gives vague feedback. You repeat the same mistakes interview after interview.
- **Generic prep wastes your time.** Grinding LeetCode for 3 months when your actual gap is behavioral storytelling. Watching 50 YouTube videos when your resume has 3 critical red flags.
- **Recruiters screen you out in under 30 seconds.** 7.4 seconds is the average resume scan time. 75% of resumes get rejected by ATS before a human sees them.
- **You don't know what you don't know.** Your resume might look fine to you, but a recruiter at Google sees 12 red flags you've never considered.
- **Confidence erodes with every unexplained rejection.** The spiral: apply → interview → get ghosted → question everything → apply again with the same flaws.

### The Emotional Hook (Fear + Empowerment)

The brand messaging uses **fear-based hooks** ("What Will Sink You") combined with **empowerment resolution** ("Fix it in 2 hours"). This is critical for organic content — you create anxiety, then immediately offer the solution.

**Pattern for every piece of content:**

1. Create a moment of "oh no, that's me" (fear/recognition)
2. Reveal the hidden problem (education)
3. Show the fix exists (resolution)
4. The fix is fast and specific (empowerment)

## 4. THE PRODUCT — COMPLETE FEATURE BREAKDOWN

### 4A. The Free Experience (Content Hook — Low Barrier to Entry)

**What you get for free:**

1. Upload resume (PDF or paste text)
2. Paste any job description
3. Select interview round type (technical, behavioral, case, finance, research/ML)
4. In ~60 seconds, receive:
   - **Readiness Score** (0-100) with risk band (High/Medium/Low)
   - **Top 3 Rejection Risks** with severity ratings and evidence
   - **Competency Heatmap Preview** (visual skill gap map)

**Marketing angle:** "You can find out your top 3 rejection risks in 60 seconds. No credit card. No signup hassle. Just the truth about your resume."

### 4B. The Full Diagnostic (13 Sections — The Premium Product)

When a user unlocks the full report (5 credits = ~$9-15), they get access to **13 distinct sections**, each one a potential content angle:

#### Section 00: Executive Summary

- Overall readiness score + risk band
- Key insights: conversion likelihood, technical fit
- Context: prior employment signal, evidence summary
- **Content angle:** "Your interview summary in one screen. Would you hire yourself?"

#### Section 01: Priority Actions

- Top 3-5 highest-impact fixes
- Time estimates (e.g., "Fix your bullet points: 20 min")
- Impact ratings
- **Content angle:** "The 3 things you need to fix before your interview (in order)"

#### Section 02: Strengths & Risks

- What's working (green cards)
- What's hurting (red cards)
- Evidence context
- **Content angle:** "Your resume has hidden strengths that recruiters never see because they're buried on page 2"

#### Section 03: Signal Strength Analysis

- 6 scoring dimensions with visual bars:
  - Hard Requirement Match (35% weight — THE biggest factor)
  - Evidence Depth (25% — quantified achievements > responsibilities)
  - Round Readiness (20% — interview prep signals)
  - Resume Clarity (10%)
  - Company Alignment (10%)
- **Content angle:** "35% of your interview score comes from ONE thing most people ignore"

#### Section 04: Hire Zone Analysis

- Shows where you stand vs. the hiring bar
- Industry average vs. your score vs. hire zone threshold
- Example: Technical hire zone = 78, industry average = 62, your score = 68
- **Content angle:** "Being GOOD isn't the same as being HIREABLE. Here's the score you actually need."

#### Section 05: Competency Map (Heatmap)

- 8 interview domains scored: System Design, Coding/Algorithms, Behavioral, Communication, Domain Knowledge, Technical Depth, Problem Solving, Leadership
- Visual heatmap — critical/warning/pass status per domain
- Seniority-aware benchmarks (Junior vs Senior expectations)
- **Content angle:** "This heatmap shows every skill gap you have for this specific job. Red = you're getting rejected for this."

#### Section 06: Recruiter Red Flags

- ALL rejection risks (not just top 3)
- Severity: critical, high, medium, low
- Evidence quotes from your resume
- "Why this matters" explanations
- **Content angle:** "Here are the 9 things that would get me rejected at Google. I had NO idea about #4."

#### Section 07: Interview Questions

- 50 personalized questions the AI predicts you'll be asked
- Likelihood percentages (e.g., "92% chance they ask this")
- Difficulty ratings
- Source context (from JD requirement, behavioral pattern, etc.)
- **Practice system:** Type your answer → get AI scoring (0-100) → get feedback with specific quotes highlighted → get the "best answer script" showing the ideal response using YOUR experience
- **Content angle:** "The AI predicted 8 out of 10 questions I actually got asked. Here's what it said about my answers."

#### Section 08: Execution Roadmap (Study Plan)

- Personalized daily study plan (1-day to 4-week plans)
- Tasks prioritized by impact
- Time estimates per task
- Categories: technical, behavioral, practice, review
- **Content angle:** "I had 7 days before my Amazon interview. This AI built me a day-by-day prep plan."

#### Section 09: Coaching Hub

- Your **Interview Archetype** (1 of 5 types):
  - "Technical Potential, Low Polish" — Great skills, poor communication
  - "Strong Theory, Weak Execution" — Knows concepts, lacks examples
  - "Resume Strong, System Weak" — Impressive resume, poor design thinking
  - "Balanced But Unproven" — Solid everywhere, standout nowhere
  - "High Ceiling, Low Practice" — Natural talent, needs reps
- Personalized coaching tips (4 per archetype)
- Improvement trajectory projection
- **Content angle:** "This AI told me I'm a 'Balanced But Unproven' candidate and gave me 4 things to do about it"

#### Section 10: Cognitive Map (Spider Chart)

- 5 cognitive dimensions visualized as radar chart:
  - Analytical Reasoning
  - Communication Clarity
  - Technical Depth
  - Adaptability
  - Problem Structuring
- Current vs. target for the role
- **Content angle:** "My spider chart showed I'm maxed on technical but my communication is tanking my score"

#### Section 11: Recruiter View Simulation

- **What the recruiter thinks** (simulated internal notes)
- First-glance reaction score
- Starred item (your standout strength)
- Internal concerns (what they'd probe in the interview)
- Phone screen questions they'd ask
- **Hiring committee debrief** (simulated):
  - One-liner verdict: "Worth advancing" / "Borderline" / "Likely pass"
  - Advocate reasons vs. pushback reasons
  - Pool percentile estimate (how you rank vs. other applicants)
- **Content angle:** "This is what a Google recruiter would write about you after reading your resume. Mine said 'On the Fence — 67% of fence candidates get rejected.'"

#### Section 12: Practice Intelligence

- **Coding Exposure Score** — How code-ready you are
- **Mock Readiness Score** — How interview-ready you are
- **Pressure Handling Index** (0-100 with 4 dimensions):
  - Time Constraint Resilience
  - Ambiguity Tolerance
  - Technical Confidence
  - Communication Under Stress
- **Consistency & Momentum Score** — Tracks your prep trajectory
- **Precision Practice Rx** — Targeted prescriptions (e.g., "Do 3 system design exercises, 45 min each, intermediate difficulty")
- **Content angle:** "My pressure handling score was 43/100. No wonder I freeze in technical interviews."

## 16. DO's AND DON'Ts FOR CONTENT CREATORS

### DO

- Show real diagnostic results (with permission or your own)
- Use the terminal-style aesthetic — it's visually distinctive
- Lead with the problem, not the product
- Make it feel like you discovered this tool, not that you're selling it
- Use real stats and numbers (they're the hook)
- Show the "before score → after score" improvement arc
- Create FOMO: "I wish I had this before my last interview"
- Use the owl mascot as a recurring character

### DON'T

- Don't make it look like a traditional ad
- Don't oversell — let the results speak for themselves
- Don't claim it guarantees job offers (it identifies risks and gaps)
- Don't ignore the free preview — it's the foot in the door
- Don't make the referral code the main focus (it should be secondary)
- Don't position it as replacing interview prep — it FOCUSES your prep
- Don't use generic stock footage — screen recordings of the actual tool are 10x more compelling
