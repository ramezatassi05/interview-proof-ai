# Viral Hook Templates

## Hook Categories

### Negative/Pain Hooks (highest engagement)
These leverage the brain's natural bias toward negative/threat information.

- "Never do [X] if you want [desired outcome]"
- "The #1 mistake [audience] make with [topic]"
- "Stop doing [common practice] — it's killing your [metric]"
- "[X]% of people fail at [topic] because of this one thing"
- "This is the worst thing you can do for your [area]"
- "If you're still doing [old method], you're falling behind"
- "Why most people will never [achieve goal]"
- "The biggest lie about [topic] no one talks about"

### Curiosity/Secret Hooks
- "Here's how [person/I] [achieved impressive result] in [timeframe]"
- "The [hidden/secret/underground] strategy behind [impressive result]"
- "I spent [time/money] learning [topic] — here's what actually works"
- "What [experts/top performers] know about [topic] that you don't"
- "The counterintuitive truth about [topic]"
- "[Number] things I wish I knew before [starting activity]"

### Contrarian/Bold Hooks
- "[Common belief] is completely wrong — here's why"
- "Unpopular opinion: [bold claim about topic]"
- "Everyone says [common advice] but the truth is [opposite]"
- "Forget everything you know about [topic]"
- "[Authority figure] was wrong about [topic]"

### Results/Proof Hooks
- "How I went from [bad state] to [good state] in [timeframe]"
- "I [achieved result] using just [simple method]"
- "This one change [increased/decreased] my [metric] by [number]%"
- "[Number] [things] that helped me [achieve result]"

### Question Hooks
- "Why does no one talk about [overlooked topic]?"
- "What if [surprising premise] was actually true?"
- "Are you making this [topic] mistake without realizing it?"

### Viral Short-Form Hook Formulas (High-Performing Patterns)

These 10 hook formulas are optimized for TikTok and short-form carousel virality. Use them as templates — fill in the blanks with InterviewProof-relevant content.

1. **"Is it even possible to ___?"** — Opens with doubt/skepticism, triggers curiosity about whether the outcome is achievable. Great for ambitious claims.
   - Example: "Is it even possible to know why you'll get rejected — before the interview?"

2. **"I did X for 30 days and this is what happened..."** — Classic transformation hook. The open-ended "what happened" creates an irresistible curiosity gap.
   - Example: "I used an AI interview diagnostic every day for 30 days and this is what happened..."

3. **"___ tier list"** — Ranking/tier list format is inherently debatable and shareable. Viewers swipe to see if they agree.
   - Example: "Interview prep methods tier list"

4. **"How to do ___ in under 60 seconds"** — Speed + value compression. Implies the viewer gets a shortcut everyone else missed.
   - Example: "How to find every red flag on your resume in under 60 seconds"

5. **"It took me X years to learn this but I'll tell you in Y seconds"** — Compresses hard-won experience into bite-sized value. The time contrast creates urgency.
   - Example: "It took me 3 years of failed interviews to learn this but I'll tell you in 45 seconds"

6. **"Never ever do this if you want to ___"** — Combines negative/pain framing with a forbidden-knowledge feel. High engagement through fear of making the mistake.
   - Example: "Never ever do this if you want to pass a behavioral interview"

7. **"I don't know who needs to hear this but ___"** — Feels personal and direct, like unsolicited but caring advice from a friend. Disarming tone.
   - Example: "I don't know who needs to hear this but your resume is getting auto-rejected and it's not your fault"

8. **"Hear me out"** — Ultra-short, conversational opener that signals an unconventional or slightly controversial take is coming. Creates instant lean-in.
   - Example: "Hear me out — LeetCode grinding is the biggest scam in interview prep"

9. **"Here's the truth about ___"** — Promises a reveal or myth-bust. Implies everyone else is lying or uninformed. Works for contrarian takes.
   - Example: "Here's the truth about why you keep getting ghosted after interviews"

10. **"Why does no one talk about ___ in [your niche]?"** — Positions the creator as someone noticing what others overlook. Triggers "finally someone said it" engagement.
    - Example: "Why does no one talk about how 75% of resumes get rejected by robots in interview prep?"

## Strong Power Words
Use these to amplify any hook:

**Urgency**: never, always, immediately, right now, today, before it's too late
**Scale**: biggest, most important, #1, only, every single
**Emotion**: crazy, insane, shocking, terrifying, game-changing
**Exclusivity**: secret, hidden, underground, little-known, overlooked
**Simplicity**: simple, easy, one thing, just, only takes
**Specificity**: exact, step-by-step, word-for-word, complete, proven

## Hook Formatting Rules for Carousels
- Slide 1 (Hook Slide) = The Cliffhanger — bold, large text, maximum 12 words
- Keep the hook as a single punchy statement or question
- Use line breaks strategically for emphasis
- The subtitle on the hook slide = the Credibility teaser (why they should keep swiping)
